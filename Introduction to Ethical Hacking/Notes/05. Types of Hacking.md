# 💻 Types of Hacking

## 📌 Introduction
Hacking can be categorized based on the target and technique used. Understanding different types of hacking helps in identifying potential threats and securing systems effectively.

---

## 🌐 1. Network Hacking
Network hacking involves gaining unauthorized access to computer networks.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Packet sniffing  
- Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attacks  
- DNS spoofing  

---

## 🌍 2. Web Application Hacking
Targets websites and web applications by exploiting vulnerabilities.

### 🔍 Examples:
- SQL Injection (SQLi)  
- Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)  
- Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF)  

---

## 📱 3. Mobile Hacking
Focuses on exploiting vulnerabilities in mobile devices and apps.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Malicious apps  
- Data leakage  
- SMS-based attacks  

---

## 📧 4. Email Hacking
Unauthorized access or manipulation of email accounts.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Phishing attacks  
- Email spoofing  
- Account takeover  

---

## 🔑 5. Password Hacking
Cracking or stealing user passwords.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Brute force attacks  
- Dictionary attacks  
- Keylogging  

---

## 🖥️ 6. System Hacking
Gaining control over computer systems.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Privilege escalation  
- Malware installation  
- Backdoor access  

---

## ☁️ 7. Cloud Hacking
Targets cloud infrastructure and services.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Misconfigured storage (S3 buckets)  
- Account hijacking  
- Data breaches  

---

## 📡 8. IoT Hacking
Exploiting Internet of Things (IoT) devices.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Smart device exploitation  
- Botnet attacks (e.g., IoT botnets)  

---

## 🧠 9. Social Engineering
Manipulating people to gain confidential information.

### 🔍 Examples:
- Phishing  
- Pretexting  
- Baiting  

---

## ⚠️ Legal Note
Hacking without permission is illegal. Always perform security testing in an authorized and controlled environment.

---

## 🧾 Conclusion
Different types of hacking target various systems and technologies. Understanding them helps cybersecurity professionals defend against potential attacks effectively.
