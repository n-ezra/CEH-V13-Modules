# ⚖️ Cyber Crimes and Laws with Important Sections

## 📌 Introduction
Cyber crimes are illegal activities committed using computers, mobile devices, networks, or the internet. These crimes may target individuals, businesses, or governments by stealing data, damaging systems, committing fraud, or violating privacy.

Cyber laws are legal frameworks created to regulate digital activities, punish offenders, and protect users online.

---

# 💻 What is Cyber Crime?

Cyber crime is any offense where technology is used as a tool, target, or medium.

### Examples:
- Hacking computer systems
- Phishing scams
- Identity theft
- Online banking fraud
- Malware attacks
- Data breaches
- Cyber stalking
- Social media impersonation

---

# 🎯 Major Types of Cyber Crimes

## 1. Unauthorized Access / Hacking
Accessing systems or accounts without permission.

## 2. Phishing
Fake emails, SMS, or websites used to steal login credentials or financial details.

## 3. Malware Attacks
Using viruses, worms, ransomware, spyware, or trojans to damage or control systems.

## 4. Identity Theft
Using another person's identity for fraud or illegal purposes.

## 5. Cyber Bullying / Harassment
Threatening, abusing, or humiliating people online.

## 6. Financial Fraud
UPI scams, credit card fraud, fake job offers, online investment scams.

## 7. Data Theft
Stealing confidential personal or company information.

## 8. DoS / DDoS Attacks
Flooding servers with traffic to stop services.

# 🇮🇳 Information Technology Act, 2000 (India)

The IT Act, 2000 is the primary cyber law in India. It provides legal recognition for electronic transactions and defines punishments for cyber crimes.

---

# 📚 Important Sections of IT Act

## Section 43
Unauthorized access, downloading data, introducing virus, damaging systems.  
**Punishment:** Compensation for damages.

## Section 65
Tampering with computer source code.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to ₹2 lakh, or both.

## Section 66
Computer-related offenses / hacking done dishonestly or fraudulently.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to ₹5 lakh, or both.

## Section 66B
Receiving stolen computer resource or communication device.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

## Section 66C
Identity theft using password, digital signature, or unique identification.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment and fine up to ₹1 lakh.

## Section 66D
Cheating by personation using computer resources.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment and fine up to ₹1 lakh.

## Section 66E
Violation of privacy by capturing/transmitting private images.  
**Punishment:** Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to ₹2 lakh, or both.

## Section 66F
Cyber terrorism.  
**Punishment:** Imprisonment for life.

## Section 67
Publishing/transmitting obscene material online.  
**Punishment:** Imprisonment + fine.

## Section 67A
Publishing sexually explicit content online.  
**Punishment:** Higher imprisonment + fine.

## Section 67B
Child-related sexual content online.  
**Punishment:** Severe imprisonment + fine.

## Section 69
Government power to intercept, monitor, or decrypt information under law.

## Section 72
Breach of confidentiality and privacy.  
**Punishment:** Up to 2 years imprisonment or fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.
